                                                  RESULTS
                                                  In total, 203 patients were assigned to receive tezepelumab and 205 to receive placebo.
                                                  At week 52, the patients who received tezepelumab had significant improvements in
                                                  the total nasal-polyp score (mean difference vs. placebo, −2.08; 95% confidence in-
                                                  terval [CI], −2.40 to −1.76) and the mean nasal-congestion score (−1.04; 95% CI,
                                                  −1.21 to −0.87) (P<0.001 for both scores). Tezepelumab significantly improved the
                                                  loss-of-smell score (mean difference vs. placebo, −1.01; 95% CI, −1.18 to −0.83),
                                                  SNOT-22 total score (−27.44; 95% CI, −32.51 to −22.37), Lund–Mackay score (−5.70;
                                                  95% CI, −6.37 to −5.03), and total symptom score (−6.96; 95% CI, −8.09 to −5.83)
                                                  (P<0.001 for all scores). Surgery for nasal polyps was indicated in significantly
                                                  fewer patients in the tezepelumab group (0.5%) than in the placebo group (22.0%)
                                                  (hazard ratio, 0.02; 95% CI, 0.00 to 0.09); there was significantly less use of sys-
                                                  temic glucocorticoids with tezepelumab (5.2%) than with placebo (19.3%) (hazard
                                                  ratio, 0.11; 95% CI, 0.04 to 0.25) (P<0.001 for both time-to-event analyses).
                                                  CONCLUSIONS
                                                  Tezepelumab therapy led to significantly greater reductions in the size of nasal polyps,
                                                  the severity of nasal congestion and sinonasal symptoms, and the use of nasal-polyp
                                                  surgery and systemic glucocorticoids than placebo in adults with severe, uncon-
                                                  trolled chronic rhinosinusitis with nasal polyps. (Funded by AstraZeneca and Amgen;
                                                  WAYPOINT ClinicalTrials.gov number, NCT04851964.)
